About 109 resultsThe Uurga, a long pole-like tool used by herders to catch horses, is a traditional lasso that allows the herder to control the animal from a distance. It is crafted from strong yet flexible wood, with the front end made from willow for pliability and toughness. The Uurga's length varies based on the landscape, with longer poles preferred in wide open steppes and shorter poles in forested or mountainous areas. In Mongolia, the Uurga is not just a tool; it symbolizes the rider's skill and spirit. Stepping over or on a Uurga is considered disrespectful, and it is placed carefully and respectfully when not in use. Herders begin training with the Uurga from a young age, learning to handle it with precision while riding at full speed. The technique requires both physical strength and refined coordination. While modern equipment is used in some places, the Uurga remains essential in daily herding tasks, especially in remote areas where horses roam freely. Uurga use is often featured in Naadam festivals, horse-catching competitions, and cultural demonstrations, showcasing the mastery of skilled riders. The Uurga represents the harmony between Mongolians and their horses, functional, elegant, and deeply respected. Amicus Travel Mongolia
