The pūkana is the facial gesture seen in kapa haka (traditional Māori performing arts), especially in haka (traditional Māori war dance). It involves the bulging of the eyes with the protruding tongue being done by tāne (men) only. Wāhine (women) bring their lips in and make a frown with their mouth with the chin slightly jutting out. The pūkana is part of the wero (challenge) and is intended to intimidate, scare and look fierce.
