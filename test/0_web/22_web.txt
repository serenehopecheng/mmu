Step-by-Step Guide to Mailing a Letter via USPS Blue Mailbox
You can mail a letter through a USPS blue mailbox (Collection Box) by following USPS addressing rules, choosing the right envelope, adding postage, and dropping it off correctly.1.

## Relevant Context 2
Choose the Right Envelope
Use a rectangular paper envelope (No. 10 or larger) for standard letters USPS.com.If your letter is large or rigid, use a flat envelope (nonrectangular, rigid, or lumpy) — these cost more and may require extra postage USPS.com.Keep the total thickness no more than ¼ inch; folding paper is allowed but must stay flat USPS.com.2. Address Your Mail Correctly
Return address (sender): Write your full name, street address, apartment/suite (if applicable), city, state, and ZIP code in the top-left corner Online Shipping Calculator.Delivery address (recipient): Write the recipient’s full name, street address, unit (if applicable), city, state, and ZIP code in the bottom center Online Shipping Calculator.Use clear, dark ink and avoid commas or periods in addresses Online Shipping Calculator.For international mail, add the country name in English on the last line of the recipient’s address Online Shipping Calculator.3.
